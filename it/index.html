Debian & others

"Talk is cheap. Show me the code." Linus Torvalds.

Since I was 8 years old I developed an interest in computers. Because of this, my father bought me a PC powered by an intel 386 processor. I also had to take classes on how to use them in elementary school. During college in Venezuela, I had some courses about quantum chemistry where I learned how to program in FORTRAN77. Later, I started meeting people from the computer sciences department and got so involved with the Linux community that I decided to become an official Debian developer. I gave several talks related to free software across Venezuela, and at some point I got a position to work as a sysadmin in a medium-size company.

I know how to program in Fortran because of my scientific career, but because of my machine learning path, I am more skilled in Python. I have basic knowledge in HTML as demonstrated with this website.
